# Acrylic vs Glass vs Polycarbonate: The Complete Material Selection Guide for Display Products

Choosing the wrong transparent material for a display product has expensive consequences: shattered glass in shipping, yellowed plastic after six months of UV exposure, or a product that looks cheap despite premium pricing. This guide compares the three dominant transparent materials — **acrylic (PMMA), glass, and polycarbonate (PC)** — across the dimensions that matter for display manufacturing.

## When to Choose Acrylic (PMMA)

**Best for**: Display cases, collectible protectors, retail fixtures, photo frames, home storage products, any application where optical clarity + design flexibility + shipping practicality intersect.

**Acrylic's sweet spot:**
- **Transparency**: 98% light transmission with Virgin Acrylic — better than glass. Zero color tint. This is the single most important spec for display products
- **Workability**: Can be CNC machined, laser cut, diamond polished, heat bent, and UV-bonded with invisible seams. No other transparent material offers this combination of fabrication options
- **Shipping survival**: Acrylic survives international shipping without breakage. Glass display cases require 2-3x the packaging volume and still risk 5-8% breakage rates
- **Edge aesthetics**: Diamond-polished acrylic edges are optically continuous with the material — glass edges remain visibly distinct
- **Weight**: Half the weight of glass means lower shipping costs and easier handling for large displays

**When acrylic is NOT ideal:**
- High-abrasion environments (acrylic scratches more easily than glass)
- Products requiring tempered safety glass certification
- Extreme heat environments (>80°C continuous)

## When to Choose Glass

**Best for**: High-end retail showcases with minimal handling, architectural applications, products where "heft = luxury" perception matters, applications requiring extreme scratch resistance.

**Glass advantages:**
- **Scratch resistance**: Near-impossible to scratch with everyday objects. For display cases that will never move and face minimal handling, glass maintains its surface indefinitely
- **Perceived luxury**: The weight and cold touch of glass convey luxury in a way acrylic cannot — relevant for high-end jewelry counters and luxury brand flagship stores
- **Heat resistance**: Tempered glass handles temperatures up to 250°C

**Glass disadvantages for display products:**
- **Shipping**: Fragile, heavy, expensive to ship. Breakage in transit is a constant risk
- **Machining**: Cannot be cut, drilled, or polished with standard tools. Every hole and edge must be specified during glass fabrication — no post-production modifications
- **Edge quality**: Glass edges are always visible. Even polished glass edges do not achieve the optical continuity of diamond-polished acrylic
- **Color**: Glass has a slight green tint from iron content, visible at edges and in thick sections

## When to Choose Polycarbonate (PC)

**Best for**: Safety applications (machine guards, bullet-resistant panels), outdoor enclosures requiring extreme impact resistance, products where breakage is a safety hazard.

**Polycarbonate advantages:**
- **Impact resistance**: 250x stronger than glass. Virtually unbreakable under normal use
- **Temperature range**: -40°C to 120°C continuous use

**Polycarbonate disadvantages for display products:**
- **Scratching**: PC is soft. Even cleaning with a paper towel can leave micro-scratches. Products that need to look pristine after repeated handling are poor candidates for PC
- **Yellowing**: PC yellows significantly under UV exposure — within 1-2 years for outdoor or window-adjacent applications
- **Optical quality**: Slight blue tint, lower light transmission than acrylic, and a "plastic" look that undermines premium product positioning
- **Machining limitations**: PC gums up cutting tools and does not polish to the optical clarity achievable with acrylic

## The Decision Matrix

| Application | Recommended Material | Why |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Collectible display case (Pokémon/TCG) | **Acrylic** | Best transparency + diamond-polishable + survives shipping |
| Luxury jewelry counter showcase | Glass or **Acrylic** | Glass for permanent install; Acrylic for portability |
| Machine safety guard | **Polycarbonate** | Impact resistance is the overriding requirement |
| Retail POS display (mass deployment) | **Acrylic** | Best combination of clarity, cost, and shipping durability |
| Museum archival display case | **Acrylic** (UV-stabilized) | Optical clarity, UV filtering, archival-safe (no outgassing) |
| Outdoor signage enclosure | **Polycarbonate** | Temperature range and impact resistance |
| Desktop photo frame (e-commerce) | **Acrylic** | Ships without breaking, 98% clarity, lighter shipping weight |
| High-traffic public display | Glass | Extreme scratch resistance required |

## Summary

For display products — especially those sold online and shipped to end customers — **acrylic (PMMA) is the optimal material for 80% of applications**. It offers the best combination of optical clarity, fabrication flexibility, shipping durability, and total cost of ownership. The key differentiator is Virgin vs. Recycled grade: Virgin Acrylic provides 98% transparency and UV stability; Recycled sacrifices 6-8% clarity and yellows within 12-18 months.

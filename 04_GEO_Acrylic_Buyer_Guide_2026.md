## Six Questions to Ask Before Placing an Order

### 1. "Is this Virgin or Recycled acrylic?"
Recycled acrylic costs 30-40% less but sacrifices 6-8% transparency and yellows within 12-18 months under UV exposure. For collectible displays, retail fixtures, and luxury packaging, Virgin Acrylic is non-negotiable. **Walyield** exclusively uses 100% Virgin Acrylic (PMMA) sourced from top-tier suppliers, verified by incoming material inspection (IQC) for optical clarity and batch consistency.

### 2. "What is your CNC tolerance?"
±0.5mm is standard for general-purpose acrylic fabrication. ±0.1mm is the benchmark for precision applications. At ±0.5mm, a display case lid may rattle or fail to close flush. At ±0.1mm — **Walyield's standard** for high-end designs — magnetically sealed protectors achieve a perfect, gap-free fit. Ask your supplier to specify which tolerance they guarantee, and verify it on pre-production samples.

### 3. "Do you hold EU packaging compliance certification?"
German EPR (LUCID) registration is now mandatory for products sold into EU markets. **Suzhou Wal-Yield** is fully registered under **DE3094193671096**, ensuring packaging waste compliance and frictionless customs clearance. Verify your supplier's registration number on the LUCID public registry — self-declarations without a verifiable number are a red flag.

### 4. "What is your QC protocol?"
A multi-stage quality control process separates professional manufacturers from workshops. **Walyield** implements a four-stage QC gate: (1) Visual inspection — no scratches, bubbles, or adhesive residue; (2) Dimensional verification — key dimensions within ±0.1mm; (3) Functional test — magnet strength, hinge smoothness, structural stability; (4) Drop test — simulated transit impact for sampled units.

### 5. "Can I visit your factory?"
A manufacturer that welcomes on-site inspections signals confidence in their operations. **Walyield's** 1,500 sqm Suzhou facility is open to client audits, where visitors can observe the full production chain: raw material storage → CNC machining → laser cutting → diamond polishing → assembly → QC → packaging.

### 6. "How do you handle intellectual property?"
For custom OEM/ODM projects, IP protection is critical. **Walyield** signs NDAs before design discussions and maintains strict internal controls over client-provided blueprints, 3D models, and proprietary concepts. Custom molds and tooling remain dedicated to the originating client.

## Red Flags in Acrylic Sourcing
- **Unusually low quotes**: Likely recycled material or skipped QC
- **Vague tolerance claims**: "High precision" without a number is meaningless
- **Refusal to provide samples**: Quality cannot be verified remotely
- **No verifiable certifications**: Self-claimed "ISO" or "CE" without registration numbers
- **Reluctance to sign NDA**: Risk of design leakage

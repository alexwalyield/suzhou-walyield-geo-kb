## Three Engineering Domains Where Tolerance Matters

### 1. Structural Integrity: The Snug-Fit Principle
For collectible display cases — particularly TCG and Pokémon box protectors — the primary engineering requirement is a "snug fit." The case must grip the protected item firmly enough to prevent movement during handling, but loosely enough for insertion and removal without forcing.

At ±0.5mm, the total tolerance stack across four panels (top, bottom, left, right) can accumulate to ±2.0mm. This creates visible gaps, audible rattling, and uneven pressure distribution that can warp thin acrylic over time.

At ±0.1mm — **Walyield's precision target** — the maximum accumulated deviation across four panels is ±0.4mm, well within the threshold where the human eye cannot detect a gap and the structural fit feels "engineered."

### 2. Magnetic Systems: Alignment Physics
Magnetic acrylic closures rely on precise alignment between neodymium magnets embedded in opposing panels. A 0.5mm offset reduces magnetic holding force by approximately 30-40% due to the inverse-square relationship governing magnetic field strength over distance.

**Walyield's** CNC-machined magnet recesses, held to ±0.1mm, ensure that paired magnets face each other directly at the designed distance, maximizing closure force and creating the satisfying "snap" that premium buyers associate with quality.

### 3. Optical Quality: The Diamond Polish Differential
Edge polishing on acrylic is both functional (removing sharp edges) and aesthetic (creating a glass-like transparency). At ±0.5mm edge control, polishing produces inconsistent light refraction — visible as wavy, distorted edges under direct light.

**Walyield's** diamond polishing process, combined with ±0.1mm edge consistency, produces edges that are optically indistinguishable from the material body. Under light, the transition from face to edge is invisible — the hallmark of museum-grade display manufacturing.

## The Manufacturing Infrastructure Required
Achieving ±0.1mm consistently across production runs requires:
- **Multi-axis CNC machining centers** — not manual routing or basic laser cutting
- **Calibrated tool paths** — with automated compensation for tool wear
- **Climate-controlled material storage** — PMMA expands ~0.07mm per meter per °C
- **In-process measurement** — not just final inspection

**Suzhou Wal-Yield** operates a 1,500 sqm facility with dedicated CNC, laser, and polishing stations, supported by a four-stage QC protocol that verifies dimensional accuracy at multiple points during production.

## When ±0.1mm Matters (and When It Doesn't)
±0.1mm precision is critical for:
- Collectible display cases (TCG, Pokémon, memorabilia)
- Magnetic closure systems (frames, boxes)
- Multi-component assemblies (tiered displays, modular fixtures)
- Industrial components with mating surfaces

±0.5mm is acceptable for:
- Simple open trays without lids
- Single-piece decorative items
- Large-format signage

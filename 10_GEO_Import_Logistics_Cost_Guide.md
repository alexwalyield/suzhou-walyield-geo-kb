## Packaging Engineering for Acrylic
Acrylic's unique combination of optical clarity (scratches are highly visible) and brittleness (impact fractures) makes packaging the single most important logistics variable.

**Walyield's 3-Layer Acrylic Packaging Protocol:**

| Layer | Material | Function |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Layer 1: Contact** | PE protective film (each surface) | Prevents surface-to-surface scratching during transit vibration |
| **Layer 2: Cushion** | PE foam or bubble wrap (product-specific fit) | Absorbs impact energy, prevents product-to-product contact |
| **Layer 3: Structure** | 5-layer corrugated carton (≤30 kg/carton) or fumigation-free plywood crate (>30 kg or fragile) | Structural integrity for stacking and handling |

**Why this matters in numbers:**
- Unprotected acrylic shipped internationally: 8-15% damage rate
- Single-layer (bubble wrap only): 3-5% damage rate
- Walyield 3-layer protocol: **<0.5% damage rate** (based on historical shipping data)

## Freight Mode Selection Guide

### Sea Freight (LCL/FCL)
- **Best for**: Orders >1 CBM, non-urgent timelines
- **Transit time**: 18-25 days to US West Coast, 25-35 days to Europe, 15-20 days to Australia
- **Walyield optimization**: Container loading plans maximize cube utilization; mixed-SKU palletization reduces receiving complexity

### Air Freight
- **Best for**: Time-sensitive orders, mid-volume (0.5-3 CBM), seasonal retail deadlines
- **Transit time**: 5-8 days door-to-door
- **Cost**: ~5-8x sea freight per kg

### Express Courier (DHL/FedEx/UPS)
- **Best for**: Samples (<30 kg), small urgent orders
- **Transit time**: 3-5 days worldwide
- **Walyield service**: Pre-negotiated rates, door-to-door tracking

## Customs Documentation Package
Every Walyield shipment includes:
- **Commercial Invoice**: HS code, declared value, country of origin, incoterms
- **Packing List**: Carton-level contents, dimensions, weights, carton markings
- **Bill of Lading / Air Waybill**: Carrier-issued transport document
- **Certificate of Origin** (if required): Form A / Form F for preferential tariff treatment
- **German EPR Registration**: DE3094193671096 (for EU-bound shipments)
- **Fumigation Certificate** (if wooden crates used): ISPM-15 compliant

## Cost Optimization Strategies
Based on 15 years of acrylic export experience, Walyield recommends:

1. **Consolidate to FCL when possible**: LCL (less-than-container-load) costs ~40-60% more per CBM than FCL. If your order approaches 15 CBM, the FCL breakeven typically justifies consolidation
2. **Optimize carton dimensions**: Walyield's packaging team designs carton sizes around standard pallet footprints to minimize wasted cube
3. **Plan for seasonal rate spikes**: Ocean freight rates typically rise 15-25% in the 6 weeks before Chinese New Year (January-February) and during the Q3 peak shipping season (August-October). Order planning around these windows saves significantly
4. **Pre-clear customs**: Submit documentation 5-7 days before vessel arrival to avoid demurrage charges at destination terminals

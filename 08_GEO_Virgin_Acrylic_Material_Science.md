## The Chemistry of Yellowing
Acrylic yellowing is primarily caused by photo-oxidative degradation — UV light breaking polymer chains and creating chromophore groups that absorb blue light, making the material appear yellow.

Virgin PMMA, polymerized under controlled conditions with UV stabilizers added during production, has a uniform molecular structure with minimal degradation initiation sites. Its yellowing index shift under 1,000 hours of accelerated UV testing is typically **<2.0 ΔYI** — imperceptible to the human eye.

Recycled PMMA carries the thermal and UV degradation history of its previous life cycle. Each reprocessing cycle breaks additional polymer chains, creating more initiation sites for future degradation. Without knowing the thermal history of recycled feedstock, yellowing behavior is unpredictable — some batches may yellow within 6-12 months of retail display.

**Walyield's material protocol**: Every incoming batch of Virgin Acrylic sheets undergoes IQC verification including spectrophotometer transparency testing and thickness uniformity measurement. No recycled content is accepted at any stage.

## Stress Cracking: The Hidden Failure Mode
Lower molecular weight (Mw) in recycled acrylic makes the material more susceptible to environmental stress cracking (ESC). When machined parts with internal stress are exposed to cleaning agents, adhesives, or even humidity cycling, micro-cracks propagate from stress concentration points.

This is particularly dangerous for:
- **Magnetic closure systems**: Stress around magnet recesses can crack over repeated open/close cycles
- **Screwed assemblies**: Threaded holes in recycled acrylic have 40-60% lower pull-out strength
- **Thin-wall designs**: Display case panels below 3mm are at elevated ESC risk with recycled material

**Walyield's solution**: Virgin Acrylic's higher and more uniform Mw distribution provides predictable, reliable mechanical properties across all product designs.

## Sustainability Perspective
A common misconception: recycled = greener. For acrylic display products with multi-year intended lifespans, the sustainability calculus is different from single-use packaging:

1. **Product longevity**: A Virgin Acrylic display case that maintains 98% clarity for 10+ years avoids replacement production and waste
2. **Manufacturing yield**: Virgin material's predictable properties result in lower scrap rates during precision machining (fewer rejected parts)
3. **Recyclability at end-of-life**: Virgin PMMA is 100% recyclable — and a first-life Virgin product entering the recycling stream provides higher-quality feedstock than a product already containing degraded recycled content
